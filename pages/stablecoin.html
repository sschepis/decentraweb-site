SDU Stablecoin — Autonomous Reserve Engine

A dollar-pegged stablecoin backed by an exogenous crypto reserve basket, with overcollateralized vault minting, autonomous health management, and a revenue waterfall that powers the entire system.

💡
The Autonomous Reserve Engine (ARE) is implemented entirely as Diamond facets (11 total), enabling atomic upgrades and trustless on-chain verification of solvency.

System Overview

The ARE produces SDU, a dollar-pegged stablecoin backed by a basket of crypto assets (USDC, BTC, ETH, stETH). A junior tranche token called RISK absorbs losses and receives surplus revenue. The system is self-powering: revenue from minting fees, liquidation penalties, and yield flows through a strict on-chain waterfall that funds insurance, keeper incentives, and peg defense before distributing surplus to RISK holders.

Core Architecture

  • SDU Token — ERC-20 stablecoin targeting $1 USD with authorized mint/burn and pause functionality
  • Reserve Vault — Holds the backing basket across 4 sleeves: Immediate Liquidity, Core Collateral, Productive Yield, and Emergency Reserve
  • RISK Token — Junior tranche ERC-20 that absorbs losses before SDU solvency is impacted; receives surplus from the revenue waterfall
  • Price Oracle — Multiple source aggregation with TWAP, median pricing, and circuit breakers for each collateral asset

Minting & Redemption

Users open overcollateralized vaults to mint SDU. The minimum collateral ratio is 150%, and liquidation triggers at 120%.

  • Vault Mint — Deposit collateral (e.g., $150 worth of ETH) and mint SDU (e.g., $100 SDU). Users manage their vaults: add/remove collateral, mint more, repay debt, or close
  • Tiered Redemption — Three modes depending on system stress:
    • Normal — Instant redemption at a narrow fee band
    • Stress — Queued with daily capacity limits
    • Severe — Batch auction redemptions at transparent discount

Health Score & State Machine

A composite health score drives every autonomous decision in the system:

📊
H = w1·C + w2·L + w3·B + w4·Y − w5·σ − w6·D
Where C = collateralization, L = liquidity depth, B = buffer health, Y = yield performance, σ = volatility penalty, D = concentration penalty. Weights sum to 10,000 bps.

The health score determines the reserve state machine:

StateConditionSystem Behavior
GrowthScore > 7,500Full emissions, surplus to RISK holders, relaxed fees
Neutral5,000 – 7,500Normal operations, moderate emissions
Defense2,500 – 5,000Emissions reduced, surplus redirected to buffer, elevated fees
RecoveryScore < 2,500Emissions halted, all revenue to buffer + losses, RISK dilution if needed

Insurance Buffer

A USDC reserve funded by the revenue waterfall, used for peg defense and keeper payments. The buffer tracks its ratio relative to SDU total supply, with a configurable target (default 5%) and minimum threshold. When the buffer falls below minimum, the revenue waterfall prioritizes replenishment before all other distributions.

Emission Controller

Incentive emissions are tied directly to system health. The effective emission rate scales linearly:

⚡
E = E_max × clip((H − H_min) / (H_target − H_min), 0, 1)
Emissions auto-halt when health drops below H_min (hard-coded safety limit). An absolute maximum rate per block is enforced and cannot be overridden by governance.

Liquidation Engine

When a vault's collateral ratio falls below the liquidation threshold (120%), anyone can partially liquidate it:

  • Partial Liquidation — Up to 50% of vault debt can be liquidated at once, preventing cascading full liquidations
  • Liquidation Bonus — Liquidators receive a 5% bonus (collateral seized exceeds debt repaid by 5%)
  • System Deficit Recovery — If the entire reserve is undercollateralized (NAV < SDU supply), RISK tokens are diluted to recapitalize the system

Revenue Waterfall

All protocol revenue flows through a strict priority-ordered on-chain waterfall. Governance cannot reorder tiers:

PriorityTierPurpose
1Replenish LossesCover any bad debt from liquidations
2Insurance BufferTop up buffer to target ratio
3Keeper PaymentsCompensate keepers for health updates, queue processing
4Peg Defense ReserveFund for active peg stabilization
5Ecosystem IncentivesLiquidity mining and protocol growth rewards
6RISK HoldersRemaining surplus distributed to junior tranche

NAV Calculation

The Net Asset Value of the reserve is computed on-chain with per-asset risk-weight haircuts:

📈
V_usable = Σ(P_i × Q_i × h_i)
Where P = oracle price, Q = balance, h = risk weight (e.g., USDC=98%, BTC=75%, ETH=70%, stETH=65%). This ensures the reported solvency is conservative.

Hard-Coded Safety Limits

These limits are enforced on-chain in the HealthMonitorFacet and cannot be overridden by governance:

Min Collateral Ratio

105% — absolute floor, even in recovery mode

Max Asset Concentration

50% — no single asset can exceed half of reserves

Min Liquidity Floor

10% — minimum share in immediate liquidity sleeve

Emission Auto-Halt

Solvency Ratio < 1.10 automatically stops all emissions

GraphQL API

The STABLECOIN schema module exposes all ARE data through the in-browser GraphQL API:

query {
  sduOverview(diamond: "0x...") {
    totalSupply price pegDeviation
    collateralizationRatio reserveNAV
    healthScore reserveState
    redemptionMode emissionsActive
  }
  sduVaults(diamond: "0x...", owner: "0x...") {
    id collateralAmount debtSDU
    collateralRatio isLiquidatable
  }
  sduHealthScore(diamond: "0x...") {
    score collateralization liquidity
    bufferHealth recommendedState
  }
}

Key Invariants

  • Overcollateralized — SDU.totalSupply ≤ ReserveVault.calculateNAV() at all times
  • Health-driven — State transitions, emissions, and redemption modes are all determined by the health score
  • Waterfall integrity — Revenue always flows losses → buffer → keepers → peg → incentives → RISK; governance cannot reorder
  • Insurance before RISK — The insurance buffer receives funding before RISK holders in every scenario
  • Partial liquidation — At most 50% of vault debt per liquidation event, preventing cascading failures
