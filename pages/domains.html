Domains & Organizations

In DecentraClick, every domain is a fully-featured organization with members, roles, governance, treasury, and subdomains.

Overview

The Domains-as-Organizations model is the foundational concept of DecentraClick. When you register a domain, you're not just claiming a name — you're creating a sovereign organizational unit with its own:

  • Member Management — Invite members, assign roles, manage permissions
  • Treasury — Multi-sig treasury with spending proposals and approval workflows
  • Governance — DAO-style governance with configurable voting strategies
  • Subdomains — Hierarchical subdomain structure for departments, teams, or projects
  • Branding — Custom logo, description, theme, and public profile
  • App Store — Install and configure applications specific to the organization
  • Services — Enable platform services (compute, database, CDN, etc.) scoped to the domain

Domain Registration

Domains are registered on-chain via the DomainRegistry smart contract. Each domain is a unique identifier that maps to an organization entity stored in Gun.js.

  1. Choose a domain name — Names must be unique and follow standard naming rules
  2. Register on-chain — The domain is registered in the DomainRegistry contract, establishing on-chain ownership
  3. Configure organization — Set visibility (public/restricted/private), branding, and initial settings
  4. Invite members — Add team members and assign roles using the RBAC system
  5. Enable services — Activate the platform services your organization needs

Organization Structure

Each organization has a hierarchical structure:

  • Owner — The wallet that registered the domain; has full administrative control
  • Admins — Delegated administrators who can manage members and settings
  • Members — Standard participants with role-based permissions
  • Subdomains — Nested organizations for teams, departments, or projects
